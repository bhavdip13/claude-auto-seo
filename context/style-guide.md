# Style Guide

> Editorial and formatting standards for all content.
> Claude Auto SEO follows these rules for every piece of content created.

---

## Grammar & Mechanics

### Punctuation
- **Oxford comma:** [Yes / No]
- **Em dash:** Use sparingly — only for strong emphasis
- **Ellipsis:** Avoid in formal content
- **Exclamation marks:** Maximum 1 per article

### Numbers
- Spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10+
- Always use numerals for: percentages (5%), money ($10), measurements
- Exception: Never start a sentence with a numeral

### Capitalization
- Title Case for: H1, H2, article titles, page titles
- Sentence case for: H3, H4, bullet points, meta descriptions
- Capitalize: [your specific product names, features, etc.]

---

## Formatting Standards

### Headings
- H1: Title case, 50–70 characters, includes primary keyword
- H2: Title case, describes the section clearly
- H3: Sentence case, supports the H2 above it

### Lists
- Use **bullet points** for: unordered items, features, tips
- Use **numbered lists** for: steps, rankings, sequences
- Minimum 3 items per list; maximum 8–10 before splitting
- Each list item: parallel structure (all start with verb, or all noun phrases)

### Bold and Italic
- **Bold:** Key terms, important warnings, first mention of concepts
- *Italic:* Titles of books/publications, technical terms on first use
- Do not bold entire sentences

### Tables
- Use for: comparisons, feature matrices, data with 3+ columns
- Always include header row
- Keep cell content concise (under 15 words per cell)

## Content Structure Templates

### Standard Blog Post
```
[Hook paragraph — problem or surprising stat]
[Brief overview of what article covers]
[H2: First major section]
  [H3: subsection if needed]
[H2: Second major section]
...
[H2: FAQ — 3-5 common questions]
[H2: Conclusion]
  [Summary of key points]
  [Single clear CTA]
```

### How-To Guide
```
[What reader will achieve + time required]
[Prerequisites or what they need]
[H2: Step 1 — [Action Verb + What]]
[H2: Step 2 — [Action Verb + What]]
...
[H2: Common Mistakes to Avoid]
[H2: Conclusion + Next Steps]
```

## CTA Standards

Every article must end with a clear next step. Use these patterns:

- **Free resource:** "Download our free [resource] →"
- **Related content:** "Next, read: [related article title] →"
- **Product/service:** "Ready to [achieve goal]? [Start free trial / Get started] →"
- **Contact:** "Have questions? [Talk to our team] →"

---

## What to Avoid

- ❌ "In today's digital landscape" (cliché opener)
- ❌ "It goes without saying" (then don't say it)
- ❌ "At the end of the day"
- ❌ "Game-changer", "paradigm shift", "synergy"
- ❌ "Delve into", "dive deep"
- ❌ Passive constructions: "It was found that..." → "Researchers found..."
- ❌ Vague claims: "significantly improves" → "improves by 47%"
